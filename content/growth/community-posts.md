## Draft A — helpful answer (slice)
> A lot of slice fixes online only talk about the club face, but for most
> people the bigger issue is an out-to-in path. A simple test: note where the
> ball *starts*. If it starts left and curves right, that's path. The headcover
> gate drill helped me a lot. (If useful, there's a free diagnosis tool that
> walks through it.)

*Use where:* golf improvement threads, only when genuinely on-topic.

## Draft B — slow-pitch pop-ups
> If you're popping up in slow-pitch, you're probably chopping down on a ball
> that's already dropping. Matching a slightly upward path to the arc fixed it
> for me — tee drill at contact height, stay through it.

*Use where:* rec softball communities, as a real answer to a real question.

## Draft C — parent question (youth hitting)
> For young hitters I'd keep it to one cue at a time and lots of tee work.
> Balance and contact first; power comes with growth. Keep it fun or they'll
> tune out.

*Use where:* youth sports parent groups, supportively.
